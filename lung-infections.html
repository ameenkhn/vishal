Understanding lung infections

Don't ignore a persistent chest infection.

While many respiratory infections resolve with routine treatment, some can progress deeper into the lungs and develop into serious conditions such as pneumonia. A cough that refuses to settle, returning fever and worsening breathlessness should never be dismissed.

Lung infections occur when bacteria, viruses, fungi or other microorganisms infect the respiratory system and cause inflammation within the lungs. Pneumonia is one of the most common and potentially serious forms, affecting the air sacs of the lungs and making breathing difficult. Prompt diagnosis is essential, especially in children, the elderly, smokers and patients with existing conditions.

Our approach

How we diagnose lung infections.

Our evaluation focuses not only on treating the infection but also understanding why it occurred, then building a treatment plan tailored to you.

Detailed clinical evaluation

A thorough assessment to identify the cause, severity and extent of the infection.

Imaging & investigation review

Chest X-ray, HRCT chest review, blood tests, oxygen assessment and sputum analysis where indicated.

Identifying underlying causes

Repeated infections may indicate an underlying respiratory condition that needs specialist attention.

Targeted medications

Treatment based on the cause and severity of infection.

Nebulisation & oxygen support

Nebulisation therapy and oxygen support when required.

Recovery & rehabilitation
