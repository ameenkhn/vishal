Lung Cancer Evaluation & Management

A cough that doesn't go away, blood in sputum, unexplained weight loss or a suspicious scan finding? Early evaluation is critical — timely diagnosis can significantly improve treatment outcomes.

Understanding lung cancer

A persistent cough should never be ignored.

Lung cancer occurs when abnormal cells in the lungs begin growing uncontrollably. It is one of the most common respiratory cancers worldwide and can affect both smokers and non-smokers.

When detected early, treatment options are often more effective and may offer significantly better outcomes. This is why persistent respiratory symptoms should never be ignored.

At Healthy Chest & Sleep Clinic, Dr. Vishal Sharma provides comprehensive evaluation, diagnosis and management guidance for patients with suspected or confirmed lung cancer.

Factors that increase risk.

Certain factors can increase the risk of lung cancer. However, lung cancer can also occur in individuals with no history of smoking.
