Airway Stenting

When narrowing, blockage or collapse of the airways makes breathing exhausting, airway stenting can be a life-changing intervention that helps restore airflow and enhance quality of life.

What is airway stenting

Restoring airflow when every breath matters.

Airway stenting is an advanced minimally invasive procedure used to keep narrowed or blocked airways open. A specially designed stent is placed inside the airway to provide structural support and maintain airflow.

You can think of an airway stent as a scaffold that helps prevent the airway from collapsing or becoming obstructed. The procedure is performed using bronchoscopy, avoiding the need for major surgery in many cases.

At Healthy Chest & Sleep Clinic, Dr. Vishal Sharma offers advanced interventional pulmonology procedures, including airway stenting, for carefully selected patients with complex airway disorders.

Why do airways become narrowed?

Understanding the underlying causes.

The airways can become narrowed or blocked for several reasons. Identifying the cause is essential to planning the right intervention.

Lung cancer

Tumours may compress or grow into the airways, restricting airflow.

Tracheal stenosis

Scarring of the windpipe following intubation, tracheostomy, infection or trauma.

Airway compression

Structures outside the airway may compress it, reducing airflow.

Benign airway disorders

Certain non-cancerous conditions can lead to significant airway narrowing.

Post-surgical complications

Some patients may develop narrowing following thoracic or airway procedures.

Airway collapse

Conditions affecting airway stability can cause the airway to collapse during breathing.

When is airway stenting recommended?

Considered when narrowing affects breathing.

Airway stenting is typically considered when significant airway narrowing is affecting breathing or quality of life. Every patient requires careful assessment before determining whether stenting is the most appropriate option.

Our approach

How we evaluate the airway.

Understanding your symptoms, respiratory conditions, previous procedures, imaging findings and lung function.

Advanced imaging review

Visualising the airway directly, measuring narrowing, assessing stability and planning intervention.

How airway stenting is performed

A step-by-step procedure.

The procedure is carefully planned and performed using advanced bronchoscopy techniques, with monitoring to ensure optimal airway function.

Pre-procedure planning

The type, size and location of the stent are carefully selected based on airway anatomy and disease characteristics.

Bronchoscopic placement

Using advanced bronchoscopy, the stent is positioned within the narrowed airway under appropriate sedation or anaesthesia.

Airway expansion

Once deployed, the stent helps maintain airway patency and improve airflow.

Post-procedure monitoring

Patients are monitored to ensure optimal airway function, with follow-up evaluations to assess long-term outcomes.

Conditions commonly managed

When stenting may be considered.

Airway stenting may be considered in patients with the following conditions. Each case requires individualised evaluation and treatment planning.

Central airway obstruction

Significant narrowing of the central airways.

Tracheal stenosis

Narrowing of the windpipe.

Bronchial stenosis

Narrowing of the bronchial airways.

Airway collapse

Tracheobronchomalacia affecting airway stability.

Cancer-related narrowing

Lung cancer-related airway narrowing.

Benign airway strictures

Non-cancerous narrowing of the airway.

Post-intubation narrowing

Narrowing following prolonged intubation.

Complex airway disorders

Other complex disorders affecting the airway.
