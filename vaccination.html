Adult Respiratory Vaccinations

Prevention is one of the most powerful forms of protection — many serious respiratory illnesses can be prevented, or their severity reduced, through timely adult vaccination.

Understanding adult vaccination

Protect your lungs before problems begin.

Many serious respiratory illnesses can be prevented, or their severity significantly reduced, through timely vaccination. For individuals with asthma, COPD, diabetes, heart disease, weakened immunity, a smoking history or advancing age, a simple respiratory infection can sometimes lead to hospitalisation and lasting complications.

Most people associate vaccinations with childhood healthcare. However, adults remain vulnerable to several serious respiratory infections, and as we age our immune system naturally becomes less effective at fighting them.

At Healthy Chest & Sleep Clinic, Dr. Vishal Sharma provides personalised vaccination guidance and preventive respiratory care to help patients stay protected, healthy and active throughout the year.

Who should consider respiratory vaccinations?

Vaccination is especially important for some.

Individuals with chronic medical conditions face an even greater risk from respiratory infections, making timely vaccination particularly valuable.

Adults above 60 years

The risk of serious respiratory infections increases with age.

Asthma patients

Respiratory infections are among the most common triggers for asthma flare-ups.

COPD patients

Infections can cause sudden worsening of symptoms and accelerate disease progression.

Smokers & former smokers

Smoking damages the lungs' natural defences, increasing vulnerability to infections.

Diabetic patients

Diabetes can impair the body's ability to fight infections effectively.

Heart disease patients

Respiratory infections can place additional strain on the cardiovascular system.

Individuals with weakened immunity

Cancer treatment, transplant recipients and immune disorders may require extra protection.

Healthcare professionals & caregivers

Frequent exposure to illness increases infection risk.

Vaccinations commonly recommended for adults

The vaccines that protect lung health.

Vaccination recommendations should always be personalised to the individual's health profile and risk factors.

Influenza (flu) vaccine

Annual vaccination helps reduce the risk of severe infection and complications such as pneumonia.

Pneumococcal vaccine

Reduces the risk of pneumonia, bloodstream infections and severe respiratory complications.

COVID-19 vaccination

An important tool for reducing the severity of illness and protecting lung health; boosters may be advised.

Additional vaccinations

Further vaccines may be advised based on age, occupation, travel, conditions and immune status.
