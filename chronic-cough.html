Chronic Cough Treatment

A cough lasting more than eight weeks is not normal. A cough that won't go away needs answers — and often points to an underlying condition that requires proper evaluation.

Understanding chronic cough

Don't just suppress the cough. Find the cause.

A cough lasting more than eight weeks is not normal. Whether it is dry, persistent or associated with mucus production, chronic cough often indicates an underlying medical condition that requires proper evaluation.

Many patients spend months trying cough syrups and antibiotics without understanding the real cause.

At Healthy Chest & Sleep Clinic, Dr. Vishal Sharma specialises in identifying the root cause of chronic cough and developing effective treatment strategies.
