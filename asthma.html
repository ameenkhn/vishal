Asthma Treatment

Constant cough, wheezing or breathlessness? Asthma can be effectively managed with the right diagnosis, treatment plan and ongoing care — so you can breathe comfortably and stay active.

Understanding asthma

Breathe comfortably. Stay in control.

If you frequently find yourself short of breath, relying on inhalers more than you'd like, or waking up at night because of coughing or wheezing, asthma may be affecting your quality of life more than you realise.

Asthma is a chronic condition in which the airways become inflamed and overly sensitive. When exposed to triggers, the air passages narrow, making it difficult for air to move freely in and out of the lungs. While asthma cannot always be completely cured, it can be successfully controlled — allowing most patients to lead normal, active and healthy lives.

At Healthy Chest & Sleep Clinic, Dr. Vishal Sharma provides comprehensive asthma evaluation and personalised treatment plans designed to help patients breathe comfortably, stay active and reduce the risk of future attacks.

Symptoms you should not ignore.

You may be suffering from asthma if you experience any of the following. Many patients live with these symptoms for years without a proper pulmonary evaluation.

Frequent coughing

Especially at night or early morning

Wheezing

A whistling sound while breathing

Shortness of breath

Walking, climbing stairs or exercising

Chest tightness

Tightness or heaviness in the chest

Seasonal breathing problems

Symptoms that come and go with seasons

Trigger-related symptoms

Dust, pollution, smoke, weather or allergies

Repeated chest infections

Persistent respiratory symptoms

Our approach

How we care for asthma.

Every patient is different. We take the time to understand your symptoms, lifestyle and triggers before building a management plan focused on long-term control.

Comprehensive assessment

Symptoms, history, allergies, exposures and previous treatments reviewed in detail.

Advanced lung function evaluation

Pulmonary function testing assesses how well your lungs are working and the severity of airway obstruction.

Personalised treatment planning

Inhaler therapy, medication optimisation, allergy management, trigger avoidance and lifestyle guidance.

Inhaler technique guidance

Incorrect inhaler use is a common reason asthma stays uncontrolled. We make sure you use yours correctly.
