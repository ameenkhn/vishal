Sleep Apnoea & Snoring Treatment

Loud snoring could be more than just a sleep problem. With the right evaluation, sleep study and treatment, sleep apnoea can be effectively managed — helping you achieve restful sleep and better long-term health.

Understanding sleep apnoea

Restful sleep. Healthier life.

Do family members complain about your snoring? Do you wake up feeling tired despite sleeping for 7–8 hours, or struggle with daytime sleepiness, poor concentration, morning headaches or unexplained fatigue? These may be signs of Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA), a common but often undiagnosed sleep disorder that can affect your heart, lungs, brain and overall health.

Sleep apnoea is a condition in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep due to narrowing or blockage of the airway. Each pause in breathing can reduce oxygen levels and disrupt normal sleep patterns, preventing the body from getting the restorative sleep it needs.

At Healthy Chest & Sleep Clinic, Dr. Vishal Sharma provides specialised evaluation and treatment for sleep-related breathing disorders, helping patients achieve restful sleep and better long-term health.

Why it should not be ignored

Untreated sleep apnoea carries real risks.

Untreated sleep apnoea has been linked with several serious health problems. Early diagnosis can significantly reduce these risks and improve quality of life.

High blood pressure

Sleep apnoea is linked with raised blood pressure.

Heart disease & stroke

Untreated apnoea increases cardiovascular risk.

Diabetes & weight gain

Associated with metabolic disturbances over time.

Poor memory & mood

Concentration problems, depression and anxiety.

Accident risk

Increased risk of road and workplace accidents.

Who is at risk?

Factors that increase your risk.

Sleep apnoea can affect both men and women and is increasingly seen in younger adults as well. You may have a higher risk if you:

Are overweight or obese

Excess weight raises the risk of airway obstruction

Large neck circumference

A larger neck can narrow the airway

Snore regularly

Habitual snoring is a key warning sign

High blood pressure or diabetes

Both are commonly associated with sleep apnoea

Smoke or drink frequently

Smoking and alcohol increase the risk

Family history

A family history of sleep apnoea or daytime fatigue

Our approach

How we care for sleep apnoea.

We take the time to understand your sleep, symptoms and risk profile before building a treatment plan tailored to the severity of your condition.

Detailed sleep assessment

We evaluate your sleep patterns, symptoms, medical history, lifestyle factors and overall risk profile.

Sleep study evaluation

A sleep study may be recommended to accurately diagnose the condition, identify breathing interruptions, oxygen fluctuations and severity.

Personalised treatment plan

Treatment recommendations are tailored according to the severity of the condition and individual patient needs.

CPAP therapy guidance

For moderate to severe apnoea, we help with device selection, mask fitting, comfort optimisation and long-term compliance.

Lifestyle & weight guidance

Lifestyle modifications play a critical role in improving symptoms and overall outcomes.
